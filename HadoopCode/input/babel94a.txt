    11	     Compiled when I became frustrated while reading magazine
    12	articles, help wanted ads and equipment for sale brochures....all
    13	pertaining to computers....where the following Abbreviations and
    14	Acronyms were used and their meanings were either not known to me
    15	or were not immediately available.  I have actually seen these
    16	combinations of letters used at one time or another in magazines,
    17	newspapers or handbooks.  They apply to personal computers,
    18	mainframes, communications, programming, networking, etc......you
    19	name it.  The glossary is free for anyone to use, but with no
    20	guarantees as to its completeness.  That is why I am now requesting
    21	your help.  Look over the definitions and let me have your
    22	feedback.  If you have ever seen letter combinations that you also
    23	did not understand, please drop me a note at the address below.
    24	
    25	     Also, if you find any errors, obsolete information or can
    26	suggest any additions or deletions, they will be most welcome.  If
    27	you can help, my thanks in advance. 
    28	
    29	     In the meantime, print out a copy of this version and keep it
    30	on your desk.  I know you will find it useful.  Updates to this
    31	glossary will be issued three times a year; in January, May and
    32	September.  You may download the latest version of BABEL from your
    33	favorite BBS or from The Internet.  If any Bulletin Board SYSOP,
    34	Club Librarian, Organization or Teacher sends me a formatted disk
    35	with a disk mailer, stamped with return postage, I will return the
    36	disk with the latest updated version of BABEL at no charge.  See
    37	APPENDIX A for additional information regarding updates.

    40	some of these, I have noted historical information.  For a brief
    41	interpretation of "smiley faces" and other informal expressions
    42	used in electronic mail messages, see APPENDIX B.
