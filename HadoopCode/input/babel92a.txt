    11	     Compiled when I became frustrated while reading magazine
    12	articles, help wanted ads and equipment for sale brochures....all
    13	pertaining to computers....where the following Abbreviations and
    14	Acronyms were used and their meanings were either not known to me
    15	or were not immediately available.  I have actually seen these
    16	combinations of letters used at one time or another in magazines,
    17	newspapers or handbooks.  They apply to personal computers,
    18	mainframes, communications, programming, networking, etc......you
    19	name it.  This list is free for anyone to use, but with no
    20	guarantees as to its completeness.  That is why I am now requesting
    21	your help.  Look over the definitions and let me have your
    22	feedback.  If you can fill in any of the blanks that I have
    23	questioned; or, if you have ever seen letter combinations that you
    24	also did not understand, please drop me a note at the address
    25	below.
    26	
    27	     Also if you find any errors, obsolete information or can
    28	suggest any additions or deletions, they will be most welcome.  If
    29	you can help, my thanks in advance. 
    30	
    31	     In the meantime, print out a copy of this list and keep it on
    32	your desk.  I know you will find it useful.  Updates to this
    33	listing will be issued three times a year; in January, May and
    34	September.  You may download the latest version of BABEL from your
    35	favorite Bulletin Board.  If any Bulletin Board SYSOP, Computer
    36	Club Librarian, Organization or Teacher sends me a formatted disk
    37	with a disk mailer, stamped with return postage, I will return the
    38	disk with the latest updated version of BABEL at no charge.  See
    39	end of listing for additional information about updates.

    42	At some of these, I have noted historical information.
