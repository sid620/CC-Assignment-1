    26	What is more confusing than Hayes AT-commands? (Init's maybe.......=*)
    27	
    28	Let's list the ones I know (the most HANDY though, left out the rest!)
    29	
    30	AT	Inform the modem that there is coming information.
    31	A/	Repeat the previous command.
    32	
    33	FROM HERE ON.....ALL COMMANDS LISTED SHOULD HAVE A "AT" DIRECTLY FORE THE
    34	COMMAND! (Example......"A" would mean "ATA"
    35	-------------------------------------------------------------------------
    36	
    37	A	Answer the phone. An "ath1" and "ata" will also work...sometimes.
    38	D	Dial the phone.
    39	H0	Hang up the phone.
    40	H1	Pick up the phone.
    41	
    42	FROM HERE ON, YOU WILL NEED A "D" AFTER "AT". (Example...."P" would be "ATDP")
    43	------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    44	
    45	P	Pulse dial the phone. (slow, and sometimes bugs)
    46	T	Tone dial the phone. (fast)
    47	
    48	MODEM SPEAKER SECTION                                     (standard AT prefix)
    49	------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    50	
    51	L0	Very low speaker volume.
    52	L1	Low speaker volume.
    53	L2	Medium speaker volume.
    54	L3	High speaker volume.
    55	
    56	M0	Shut up the speaker!
    57	M1	Speaker on, but off while carrier/connect.
    58	M2	Turn on the speaker.
    59	
    60	That is the most useful I know. There are SEVERAL others, but we won't
    61	cover those........................yet. There is also SEVERAL other
    62	vendor-specific at-commands.....
