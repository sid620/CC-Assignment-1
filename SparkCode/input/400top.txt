     7	   CONFLICTS WITH 8514A COMPATIBLE VIDEO BOARDS
     8	
     9	     The IBM 8514a and compatible video boards will conflict with any modem
    10	     or faxmodem you configure as COM4. This is because 8514a and
    11	     compatible video boards use the I/O address 2E8 which is the I/O
    12	     address used by COM4.
    13	
    14	     If you use an 8514a or compatible video board, DO NOT set your modem
    15	     or faxmodem to COM4. Neither the video board nor the modem will
    16	     operate reliably.
    17	
    18	     This also applies to video boards based on the S3 chipset.
    19	
    20	   S3-BASED VIDEO BOARDS
    21	
    22	     Video boards based on the S3 86C911 controller will use the I/O
    23	     address 2E8 (but won't use IRQ 3). This means you can't address your
    24	     modem as COM4.
    25	
    26	     The S3 controller has several features derived from the IBM 8514/a
    27	     adapter. Along with these features comes inherent issues with 8-bit
    28	     devices.
    29	     The 86C911 is a 16-bit device that uses x2E8h, x6E8, xAE8, xEE8h to
    30	     access its enhanced more register; all 16 bits are decoded. Since the
    31	     AT bus decodes only the lower 10 bits of the I/O address, conflicts
    32	     will occur between the 86C911 and other devices using the same
    33	     addresses.

   100	   MODEM WON'T AUTOANSWER WHEN SLS ENABLED
   101	
   102	     If the SatisFAXtion board is set for "Shares phone line, mostly fax
   103	     calls", "shares phone line, mostly voice calls", or "Shares phoneline,
   104	     has a distinctive ring", modem callers MUST include the characters  @#
   105	     at the end of their dialing string. The "@" command tells the calling
   106	     modem to wait for a period of silence (30 seconds by default). The "#"
   107	     character tells the answering SatisFAXtion board the call is from a
   108	     modem.
   109	
   110	     Otherwise, if no CNG tone is detected, the SatisFAXtion board will
   111	     assume the call is a voice call and ring the downline phone. If users
   112	     truly need the board to arbitrate between fax and modem calls without
   113	     user intervention, then they must set the SLS setting for "has its own
   114	     phone line".

   142	   TROUBLESHOOTING SEND/RECEIVE PROBLEMS
   143	
   144	     If you're having trouble sending or receiving, here are some thing you
   145	     can try:
   146	
   147	         Always load a disk cache program BEFORE loading CASMGR.
   148	
   149	         Always load network software BEFORE loading CASMGR and FAXPOP.
   150	
   151	         Do not load anti-virus software in TSR mode.
   152	
   153	         If you use the STACKER program to increace your disk storage
   154	          space, always load the STACKER drivers BEFORE loading SATISFAX.SYS
   155	          in your CONFIG.SYS file.
   156	
   157	         If you use QEMM 6.0, disable the STEALTH feature. Later versions
   158	          may or may not have this STEALTH mode problem fixed.

   230	   I/O AND IRQ CONFLICTS
   231	
   232	     When two devices share the same I/O address or IRQ (interrupt), one or
   233	     the other (or both) devices may not work correctly.  To eliminate the
   234	     potential for conflicts, make sure each device you add to your
   235	     computer occupies a unique I/O address and IRQ.
   236	
   237	     Standard COM (serial) Port Address/IRQ Settings
   238	
   239	                   I/O
   240	            Port   Address   IRQ
   241	            ----   -------   ---
   242	            COM1     3F8      4
   243	            COM2     2F8      3
   244	            COM3     3E8      4
   245	            COM4     2E8      3
   246	
   247	     Intel faxmodems and modems come with a program called TESTCOM.EXE that
   248	     can tell you what I/O address and IRQs are in use. Change to the disk
   249	     or directory containing TESTCOM.EXE and enter this command:  TESTCOM
   250	     /S
   251	
   252	     If TESTCOM reports that only COM1 is active, set the modem to COM2.
   253	     If TESTCOM reports both COM1 and COM2 active, disable the COM2 port in
   254	     your computer (if possible) so that you can set the modem to COM2.
   255	     See the user's guide for your computer to find out how to disable a
   256	     COM port.

   271	     If you're using both COM1 and COM2 or can't disable COM2, you may be

   273	     COM2.  A mouse and a modem will not successfully share an IRQ.  A

   276	     The Intel SatisFAXtion faxmodems, Intel internal 2400 baud modems, and

   281	     COM4   2E8   IRQ 2  (works in 8088/8086-based (PC/XT) computers)
   282	
   283	     NOTE:      Before configuring your modem for COM3/IRQ 5 or COM4/IRQ 2,
   284	                make sure your communications software supports these ports.
   285	
   286	     NOTE:      Never use IRQ 5 in 8088/8086-based computers because IRQ 5
   287	                is used by your hard drive. Never use IRQ 2 in 286/386/486-
   288	                based computers because IRQ 2 is used to "cascade" the two
   289	                interrupt controllers.
